# Toggle Group — story plan

Tier 3 floor for toggle-group: 6–10 stories. 8 planned. Derived from `brief.md` §3 (when to use), §6 (behavior), §7 (a11y contract), §8 (props), §10 (pitfalls). Includes the required composite-keyboard coverage (arrows/Home/End/RTL) called for by the toolbar cluster's shared roving-focus engine.

| # | Name | Renders | Play function? | DoD section(s) served |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `Hero` | The kept docs hero demo as-is — single-select text-alignment ToggleGroup with icon Toggles | No | Hero example; identity strip |
| 2 | `Multiple` | The kept docs "Multiple" demo — `multiple` prop, bold/italic/underline independently toggleable | Yes — click two items, assert both stay pressed simultaneously; click one again, assert only it unpresses (brief §6, `ToggleGroup.test.tsx` "prop: multiple") | Examples (kept functionality demo, Decision 8); Behavior |
| 3 | `SingleSelectClearable` | Single-mode (`multiple={false}`) ToggleGroup, emphasizing that clicking the pressed item again deselects it entirely (contrast with RadioGroup) | Yes — click item A (pressed), click item A again, assert nothing is pressed; click item B, assert only B is pressed (brief §4, §6) | When not to use + alternatives (the RadioGroup boundary, `_clusters/binary-controls.md`) |
| 4 | `CompositeKeyboardNavigation` (required) | A 3+ item ToggleGroup wired for a play function exercising the full composite-keyboard contract | Yes — Tab in (single stop), ArrowRight/ArrowLeft (or Down/Up for a vertical variant) move roving focus, confirm looping at the ends, Home moves to first, End moves to last, confirm cross-axis arrows are no-ops (brief §6, §7, `ToggleGroup.test.tsx` parametrized suites + Home/End tests) | Accessibility contract (full keyboard table); the single most important test-bearing story for this component |
| 5 | `RTLKeyboardNavigation` | Same composite wired inside a `DirectionProvider direction="rtl"`, horizontal orientation | Yes — assert ArrowLeft moves to next item and ArrowRight to previous (direction flipped from LTR), per brief §6/§7's RTL row | Accessibility contract (RTL keyboard row); a11y contract honesty (this is a real, tested behavior difference, not a stylistic footnote) |
| 6 | `DisabledGroupAndItem` | One story showing both `<ToggleGroup disabled>` (cascades to all) and an individually `disabled` Toggle inside an otherwise-enabled group | Yes — assert whole-group disable via the `disabled` prop on Root; assert one disabled item is excluded from the roving-focus tab sequence while siblings remain reachable (brief §6, §7) | Behavior (disabled cascade); a11y contract (disabled-item discoverability, cross-referenced against Toggle's own hardcoded `focusableWhenDisabled: false`) |
| 7 | `InsideToolbar` (composition/boundary story) | A ToggleGroup nested inside `<Toolbar.Root>`, recreating the toolbar hero demo's alignment cluster, explicitly demonstrating that Home/End do **not** move focus in this configuration (unlike story 4) | Yes — press Home/End inside the Toolbar-hosted group, assert focus does **not** jump to first/last (contrast with story 4's standalone assertions) — the single most citable, source-verified nuance in the brief (§5/§6/§9, PR #3971) | Anatomy & composition (Toolbar-hosting dual render path); this is exactly the kind of honest, evidence-backed "gotcha" story the research brief exists to surface |
| 8 | `SegmentedControlViewSwitcher` (real-world-flavored recreation) | A view-mode switcher (grid/list/table icons, single-select) styled as a common "segmented control" archetype named explicitly in the docs metadata keywords | No (static composition demo, or a light click-to-switch-view illustration) | When to use (§3); Real-world patterns observed placeholder |

Notes:
- Story 7 is the single highest-value story in this plan: it is the only place in the whole four-component batch where a subtle, easily-miscommunicated behavioral asymmetry (Home/End working standalone but not Toolbar-hosted) can be *shown*, not just described in prose — recommend prioritizing it if story budget must be trimmed.
- No real-world recreation stories with Phase D interaction data planned (no corpus exists yet for toggle-group — brief §11); story 8 is a plausible-archetype composition demo, not a corpus recreation.
